A tech stack is the set of technologies used to develop an application, including programming languages, frameworks, databases, front-end and back-end tools, and APIs. Choices with your tech stack can have significant downstream effects, including the kinds of integrations you can build and the skills you'll need to hire for. Product companies usually include tools that measure product and user analytics. Investing in the various elements of your tech stack is a crucial step to finding success as a software company, since your tech stack gives your product team the tools it needs to build and maintain your product, and to make sure it continues to meet customer needs.
Here are the basic categories along with some popular options for each: Operating systems and programming languages – You’ll choose these based on the environment you’re most comfortable developing in as well as the type of application you want to optimize for.
